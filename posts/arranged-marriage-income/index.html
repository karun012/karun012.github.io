Arranged Marriages - How much money do you make?

January 10, 2022 2-minute read
life

This is a satire. The only intention here is to make fun of this whole process. Take it with a grain of salt and don’t be offended.

In the world of arranged marriages the first fairly common question you’ll get asked is - How much money do you make? The big thing about income is - because I make X, I’m only open to speaking to someone who makes something greater than X. This happens both to women and men.

While I find this question to be a red flag and I don’t talk further if I hear that right at the beginning, I have decided to try my best to help people out to better figure out financial status of a potential match. Income will not tell you much about a person. All the income might be going towards their current debt or their wine collection or some other hobby. It’s also not a very interesting financial indicator until you factor in taxes, living expenses, etc.

For a very long time everyone has been asking just for income. Instead, you should get creative and analyze financial health/status using more interesting metrics -

Net-worth without illiquid assets - for those of you who don’t know, illiquid assets cannot be converted to cash very easily. They may have a giant island lying around somewhere or a Ferrari California, but it’s not easy to sell. So factor that into your calculations. Most hobby related assets will also be illiquid

Relative wealth index - this will tell you how much the person is using out of their income to build their current lifestyle using consumable assets. Net worth / consumable assets. They can increase it by increasing their net worth or reducing their consumable assets. This will help you find out how easily lifestyle can be upgraded

Savings-to-income-ratio - this is how much they save every month from the income they make. It’s a better indicator of how quickly and easily you can change the relative wealth index

Total passive income - in case you wish the other person to quit their job, this will be a useful metric to know because regular monthly income will not be around anymore :P

Thanks to https://bassamsalem.com/2013/09/22/personal-finance-kpis/ - for some of these KPIs

I urge all matrimonial sites to add these KPIs instead of annual income :P
